Free Walking Tours Of Bath

Bath's free walking tours offer a fantastic (and free!) way to explore the city and discover its hidden gems. Packed with information and covering all the must-see sights, these tours run twice daily, with additional options during peak season.

No.1 Royal Crescent

Standing before the Royal Crescent, with its majestic curved architecture, is a truly unforgettable experience. While most buildings are private homes, No. 1 offers a unique glimpse into the past. Step inside this late 18th-century time capsule, complete with costumed guides who vividly narrate the building's history.

Roman Baths

Ever wondered why Bath is called Bath? The answer lies in its most famous attraction - the Roman Baths! A must-see for any visitor, these baths were revered for their supposedly healing waters and became a cornerstone of the city's development. Beyond the impressive main bath, explore the captivating ruins hidden beneath the ground, discover ancient artifacts, and delve into a captivating modern retelling of Bath's ancient story. Feeling adventurous? You can even sample the pump water yourself!

Climb the tower at Bath Abbey

Scaling the Abbey tower is a must-do for clear-weather adventurers! For a mere £6, embark on a guided "tower tour." Knowledgeable guides will fill you in on the history while you conquer the challenging 212-step climb. Once you reach the bell ringing area, the adventure continues across the cathedral roof before a final ascent. The panoramic views from the top are truly breathtaking, offering a unique perspective of Bath that will leave you speechless!

Hop On A Boat In Bath

Take a leisurely cruise with Bath City Boat Trips or Pulteney Cruisers. While soaking in the scenic views of Pulteney Bridge and the Weir, you'll also be treated to a fascinating historical commentary, enriching your understanding of the city.

4 Days Itinerary

We've crafted a unique 4-day itinerary specifically designed for those seeking adventure without breaking the bank. This itinerary outlines exciting activities and hidden gems, all while keeping your daily budget remarkably low!

  • Get Started

  • Day 1

    Roman Grandeur & Bustling Markets

    Budget: AED 71

    Consider purchasing a Bath Bus Hop-on Hop-off Pass for discounted travel

    9:00AM-4:00PM

    Morning: Explore the iconic Roman Baths (entrance fee: £23 - approximately AED 104). Marvel at the Great Bath and learn about Roman bathing culture. Tip: Consider purchasing a Bath City Pass for discounted entry to major attractions.
    Afternoon: Wander through the bustling Bath Guildhall Market, a haven for local crafts, souvenirs, and delicious street food options (around £5-10 - approximately AED 23-46).

  • Day 2

    Literary Delights & Scenic Walks

    Budget: AED 54

    Look out for free walking tours!

    9:00AM-4:00PM

    Morning: Immerse yourself in the world of Jane Austen at the Jane Austen Centre (entrance fee: £12 - approximately AED 54). Learn about her life and literary works.
    Tip: Check for free exhibitions or events happening during your visit.
    Afternoon: Enjoy a scenic walk along the Bath Skyline path, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city (free). Pack a picnic lunch (around £5 - approximately AED 23) to enjoy along the way.

  • Day 3

    Hidden Gems & Thermal Delights

    Budget: AED 90

    Pack a refillable water bottle!

    9:00AM-4:00PM

    Morning: Explore the quirky and fascinating Postal Museum (entrance fee: £10 - approximately AED 46), showcasing the history of postal services and communication.
    Afternoon: Take a relaxing dip in the Thermae Bath Spa's hot springs (prices vary, expect around £20-30 for entry - approximately AED 71-136). Experience the natural thermal waters that have drawn visitors for centuries.

  • Day 4

    Architectural Gems & Farewell Stroll

    Budget: AED 36

    Pack snacks for in-between meals!

    9:00AM-1:00PM

    Morning: Admire the architectural beauty of Bath Abbey, a stunning example of Gothic architecture (free entry, donations appreciated). Afterwards, visit the Pulteney Bridge, a unique bridge with shops lining both sides.
    Afternoon: Enjoy a leisurely stroll through the Royal Crescent, a magnificent crescent-shaped terrace with stunning Georgian architecture. Take in the final sights of Bath before your departure.
